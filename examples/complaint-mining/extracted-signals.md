# Extracted Signals — Complaint Mining

> What Midas found in 2 weeks of friend-group complaining.

This example is the purest demonstration of **Lens 2 — Demand Gap Identification**. The entire input is demand gaps in their rawest form: frustration + pain + explicit willingness-to-pay. Midas's job here is to recognize that a dozen separate-feeling complaints are actually three to five underlying business concepts.

---

## Stage 1 — INGEST

```
Input type: group_chat_whatsapp
Volume: ~60 complaint/wish messages across 14 days
Participants: 8 people (Sofia, Tara, Dan, Priya, Max, and 3 quieter members)
Demographic: mid-30s, mid-sized U.S. city, mostly parents with young kids, professional jobs
Ready for Stage 2: yes
```

## Stage 2 — SCAN (6-lens extraction)

### Lens 2 — Demand Gap Identification (primary lens for this input)

**Raw Signal 2.1 — Backup roster for recurring local services**
> Evidence from March 23 (Sofia): "i don't need a marketplace. i need a roster."
> Evidence from March 27 (Sofia): "the backup roster thing for EVERYTHING."
> Evidence from April 3 (Priya): "you need a pre-built trusted network."
> Evidence from April 4 (Sofia): "nanny share groups are black magic. how do you even get into one?"
> Evidence from April 5 (Sofia, Tara, Priya, Dan — all four): "same." — 5 yeses from 8 in response to Max's "someone build this please"
>
> Strength: ⭐⭐⭐⭐ (this is beyond the normal 3-star ceiling)
>
> This signal is corroborated by **5 independent people** over a 14-day period. The demand is so well-articulated that by the second week, the participants are naming the gap themselves. Multiple willingness-to-pay statements: "$50/mo" and "I would pay for that." This is not a hypothesis — this is a focus group that has already self-organized.

**Raw Signal 2.2 — Summer camp discovery and pricing transparency**
> Evidence from March 25 (Tara): "there should be an app that just lists every camp in a city with real-time availability and pricing. it would be the simplest app ever."
> Evidence from March 25 (Sofia): "I WOULD PAY FOR THAT"
> Evidence from March 30 (Tara): $3,200 penalty for missing an email about early-bird pricing the YMCA never sent
> Evidence from April 2 (Sofia): "summer child care should not be this hard and yet it destroys a week of my productivity every june trying to plan it"
>
> Strength: ⭐⭐⭐
>
> Three independent parents describing the same summer-camp-discovery problem. One has already paid a $3,200 penalty (explicit dollar pain). Explicit WTP statement from Sofia. Recurring annual pain ("every june").

**Raw Signal 2.3 — Finding a good small-business accountant**
> Evidence from March 24 (Dan): "anyone know a good accountant for small business?"
> Evidence from March 24 (Priya): "good luck lmao. i've been looking for 6 months"
> Evidence from April 1 (Dan): still hasn't found one — "i'm actually screwed now for tax deadline"
> Evidence from April 1 (Priya): found one through neighbor recommendation, booked until June
>
> Strength: ⭐⭐⭐
>
> Two independent people with the same 6+ month search problem. One is now panicked about the tax deadline. Both eventually solve the problem only through personal recommendation. The "6 months to find an accountant" friction is a clearly documented pain with clearly documented cost of delay (late filing penalties, operational paralysis).

**Raw Signal 2.4 — Car inspection / regulatory navigation blindspot**
> Evidence from March 26 (Max): 4 inspection shops quoted 3+ weeks wait
> Evidence from March 26 (Max): there's a 30-day extension rule "but you have to know to ask for it and like nobody tells you"

>
> Strength: ⭐⭐
>
> Single-person-source on the car inspection itself, but it's reinforced by the broader pattern of "regulatory information nobody can find." This signal is really a **local knowledge access gap** — the kind of information that a local-concierge service would sell.

**Raw Signal 2.5 — Gift-finding for adults**
> Evidence from March 28 (Priya): "i hate going to those mall shops and wandering around lost for 45 min looking for a gift"
> Evidence from March 28 (Sofia): "yeah gift-finding is its own hell"
>
> Strength: ⭐
>
> Weak signal. Only 2 people mentioned it, and it's the kind of complaint that exists in every friend group without representing a differentiated market gap. Amazon and Etsy already serve this. Store and wait.

### Lens 1 — Money Signal Detection

**Raw Signal 1.1 — Home service pricing shock**
> Evidence from March 29 (Dan): "$900 and $1400. FOR GUTTERS."
> Evidence from March 29 (Max): "pricing on any kind of home service has gotten insane"
> Evidence from March 29 (Sofia): same 2-3 handymen used by everyone in the neighborhood, all booked
>
> Strength: ⭐⭐
>
> Money is moving at high prices in home services. This isn't just a complaint — it's a price signal that suggests the labor supply in local trades is structurally short relative to demand. Every person who works in this market is charging peak prices.

**Raw Signal 1.2 — $3,200 YMCA mistake**
> Evidence from March 30 (Tara): $3,200 in unnecessary spend because YMCA never sent an early-bird pricing email
>
> Strength: ⭐⭐
>
> Concrete dollar loss in a specific vertical (summer camps). The willingness of families to swallow a $3,200 loss rather than litigate or cancel reveals that summer care is **non-substitutable** once you're committed. That inelasticity is where pricing power lives.

### Lens 3 — Arbitrage Window Detection

**Raw Signal 3.1 — Information asymmetry in local services**
> Evidence from April 1 (Dan): "i've been googling for 2 hours and every article is written by a bot selling me something"
> Evidence from April 1 (Tara): "literally the internet is unusable for actual answers now"
> Evidence from April 1 (Max): "the ONE thing i miss about the old internet is when you could google something and get an actual answer"
>
> Strength: ⭐⭐⭐
>
> This is information arbitrage. The gap between "real local knowledge" and "SEO-optimized AI-generated spam" has become enormous. Anyone who can produce trustworthy local answers (curated, human-verified, no monetization-by-ad) can charge for it because the free alternative has gotten structurally worse. This is a macro trend converging with a micro opportunity in one chat.

---

### Lens 4 — Skill-to-Revenue Bridge

**Raw Signal 4.1 — The group itself is the product**
> Evidence from April 3 (Priya): "yeah the version that would work is: 10 people in your neighborhood who all use the same vetted sitters, handymen, accountants, dog walkers. you don't need an app. you need a pre-built trusted network."
> Evidence from April 3 (Tara): "just send me the list priya. i'll pay you"
>
> Strength: ⭐⭐⭐
>
> This is a crucial signal Midas almost missed. The friends in this chat are, collectively, an existing trusted network — they just haven't realized they could operationalize it. One of them (whichever is most organized) could literally create a shared document of "every vetted provider any of us uses" and charge a small membership fee to expand the circle to friends-of-friends. **The skill is group curation, and it already exists organically.**

---

### Lens 5 — Network Monetization Path

**Raw Signal 5.1 — The chat IS a monetizable network**
> Evidence: the chat itself. 8 people share providers informally already.
>
> Strength: ⭐⭐
>
> Any one of the 8 members of this chat is one Google Doc away from running a paid local-concierge service for their immediate social network. The network path requires no recruiting because the network exists.

### Lens 6 — Behavioral Leverage Point

**Raw Signal 6.1 — Annual summer camp logistics as a recurring ritual**
> Evidence from April 2 (Sofia): "summer child care should not be this hard and yet it destroys a week of my productivity every june"
>
> Strength: ⭐⭐
>
> Every parent in the chat loses ~1 week every June to summer camp logistics. That's an unacknowledged annual tax that the chat collectively pays. A service that absorbs that week (intake form in March, registered kids by mid-April, backup plans assigned, schedule shared) would be bought enthusiastically if the price is under 2 days of lost income per family.

## Stage 3 — CROSS-REFERENCE

The most important cross-reference in this example is *within the chat itself*:

Every signal above keeps converging on the same underlying concept: **a trusted, hyper-local human network for recurring service needs**. The phrases that appear in different messages from different people over different days are all variations on the same theme:

- "i don't need a marketplace. i need a roster." (Sofia, March 23)
- "the backup roster thing for EVERYTHING" (Sofia, March 27)

**Midas counts this as 7 independent signals converging on one opportunity over 14 days.** Confidence jumps dramatically.

## Why This Example Matters

The Slack example showed Midas extracting signals from *low-signal* noise (a normal work week). The photo example showed Midas extracting signals from *moderately dense* noise (a curated week of phone photos). **This example shows what happens when the input is already filtered for complaints — the signal density goes through the roof.**

Lesson for the user: if you only ever feed Midas one type of input, feed it your complaint-rich conversations. Humans don't complain casually about things they don't care about, and repeated complaints from multiple people are the most honest market research in existence.

When 5 of 8 people in a chat say "same" to "someone build this please" — **that is the market speaking.**
