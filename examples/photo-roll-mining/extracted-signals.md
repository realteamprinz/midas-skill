# Extracted Signals — Photo Roll Mining

> What Midas found in 30 unfiltered phone photos from one week.

Photos are signal-sparse per-item but extraordinarily powerful for cross-referencing. Watch how Midas identifies three converging threads buried in what looks like a normal week of phone photography.

**Raw Signal 1.2 — User's own woodworking spend**

> Strength: ⭐⭐

### Lens 3 — Arbitrage Window Detection

**Raw Signal 3.1 — Pricing gap between hobby and commission woodwork**
> Evidence (Photo 11): hand-made cutting boards sell for $65 at the farmer's market
>
> Evidence (Photo 22): raw white oak costs $8.49/bf
>
> Evidence (Photo 27): finish carpenters charge $45–85/hr
>
> Evidence (Photos 17, 18): a stranger has already offered the user ~$2,800 for a kitchen island commission after seeing work by a mutual connection
>
> Strength: ⭐⭐⭐
>
> Classic skill/packaging arbitrage. The user's labor produces output that the market is already pricing at retail levels ($65 cutting boards, $2,800 islands, $45–85/hr carpentry). The user has been assessing their own output at zero because it's "just a hobby." **That is the entire arbitrage.** No matter how slow the user builds, every hour spent is generating real market-rate economic value.

**Raw Signal 3.2 — Subcontracting arbitrage**
> Evidence (Photo 26): Morrison Woodworks turns down 2–3 commissions per week due to backlog
>
> Strength: ⭐⭐⭐
>
> This is pure capacity arbitrage. Morrison Woodworks has pre-qualified demand they can't fulfill. The user has capacity. The market rate is known. The introduction has already happened (Photo 25–26, 29). All that's missing is the formal agreement.

## Stage 3 — CROSS-REFERENCE

**Internal cross-references within this input (without loading prior evolution.jsonl):**

The photos themselves corroborate each other in powerful ways. This is a rare single-input run where three or more lenses converge within the same batch:

| Lens combination | Convergence point |
|---|---|
| Money Signal + Demand Gap + Skill Bridge | Local construction dollars + hobbyist woodworker + known carpentry shortage = the user is in the exact right geography with the exact right skill while capital is being spent on exactly the craft they can supply. |
| Skill Bridge + Network Path + Behavioral Leverage | User's workshop + Morrison Woodworks subcontracting offer + inbound kitchen island lead = the business plan is already executing without being named. |
| Arbitrage + Money Signal + Demand Gap | $65 cutting boards + $8.49/bf lumber + $2,800 commission offers + $45–85/hr carpentry rates = well-documented market prices already in the input, with user-level inputs priced at near-zero opportunity cost. |

**This is the cleanest convergence in any example in this skill.** Because all the signals come from a single week of photos, they're effectively independent of each other as data types (the construction sites don't know about the cutting board, the Morrison Woodworks DM doesn't know about the commission email), which pushes cross-lens confidence significantly higher.

## Why This Example Is Important

The Slack mining example showed Midas extracting signals from work noise. This example shows Midas extracting **a complete business hidden in plain sight** from *personal* noise. The user has already built the business without noticing. The 30 photos, read in the right order with the right lenses, describe a fully-loaded woodworking commission practice ready to launch on Monday morning.

The lesson: **the signals aren't subtle. They're just invisible without the lenses.** Every photo in the raw input is something the user took and then forgot about. None of them felt meaningful in the moment. All of them together form an unambiguous conviction signal.
